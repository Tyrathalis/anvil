## Broke / surprised me

- **The reconciliation dive found a real engine-interaction class: silent
  illegal-block discards.** Min-blocker *restrictions* (menace; Troll of
  Khazad-dûm's "except by three or more") pass the D5 block realizer —
  whose repair covers *requirements* only — and the engine silently
  discards the illegal block downstream: no census record, the blocker just
  doesn't block. Extends D5's "engine never validates AI blocks":
  restrictions ARE enforced, silently, post-declaration. The §6c derivation
  catches this class; census can't. Worklist gains the serve-side fix
  (realizer learns `MinMaxBlocker`, pairs with block-drop re-ask).
- Census combat `dropped`/`forced` counts the realizer's *internal repair
  round-trips*, not net intent deviation (g443: drop+force restored the
  declared attack exactly) — the derivation's intent-vs-outcome basis is
  the semantically correct penalty accounting, and the gate wording was
  amended accordingly (priority exact; combat diagnostic with attribution).
