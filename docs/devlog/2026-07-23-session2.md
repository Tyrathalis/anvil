## Method

Full-vis critic evaluated at the first obs-carrying decision of every turn,
model's perspective, over both 1,000-game arms of the run-7b closing read
(2,000 games, 1,109 W / 891 L). ~45.5K windows, ~220 s/critic on GPU. Run
twice with independently-trained critics — the on-policy `run7b/iter-014/
critic` and the heuristic-corpus `d4-critic-fullvis` — **every headline
number agrees within ~1pp between them**; conclusions below are the i14
values. Records: `data/runs/early-doom-run7b-{i14,d4crit}/`
(traces.jsonl, summary.json, curation.jsonl).

## Findings

- **Luck-locked loss fraction is small: the ceiling is NOT binding.**
  Strictest read (never reached v ≥ 0.50 at any turn): 39.2% of losses.
  Conservative read (never even v ≥ 0.30): 17.8%. The corresponding
  ceilings on this eval: 0.826 / 0.920. The recipe's 0.5530 sits ~27pp+
  below the strict ceiling — **policy improvement is nowhere near a luck
  wall; scaling remains a live lever.**
- **Control is clean:** the same doom criterion fires on only 0.4–1.6% of
  WINS (false-doom), so the loss-side fractions aren't criterion noise.
- **Calibration supports the read:** reliability bins within ~2–5pp of
  realized (mid-bins slightly underestimate model wins — consistent with
  critics trained on weaker play); AUC by absolute turn 0.74 (t1–3) →
  0.90 (t17+). The doom criterion is max-over-trajectory, so it doesn't
  hang on any single early window.
- **Symmetric luck read:** only 41.9% of wins were never-trailed — games
  swing; consistent with the large addressable pool.
- **Curation signal delivered: 531 addressable losses** (59.6% of losses:
  model held v ≥ 0.5 at some point, lost anyway), each with its largest
  value-crash window, ranked (`curation.jsonl`). 185 have single-turn
  collapses ≥ 30pp; top of the list are 0.7–0.95 → ~0.1 one-turn cliffs
  (combat/interaction shaped). Spread over 102 model decks — no
  deck-pathology concentration. Provenance complete per row: store, game
  index, seed, seat, crash turns, decks — Grindstone drill seeds ready.
