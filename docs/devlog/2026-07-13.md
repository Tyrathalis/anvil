## Done

- **Labeling pilot COMPLETE** (`d4-rollout-pilot-20260712-131049`, paused
  for a reboot mid-run and resumed with zero loss — the chunk mechanism's

  predictor against the true mainline outcome at the same 1,842 windows;
  record `data/runs/d4-pilot-signal-comparison.json`): **rollout means
  out-rank the full-vis critic in every turns-from-end band** — AUC 0.864
  vs 0.842 overall, 0.970 vs 0.915 at 5–8 (+5.5pp), 0.858 vs 0.849 at
  9–16, 0.710 vs 0.690 at 17+. Worse BCE (0.543 vs 0.485) = K=8
  quantization, not signal — ranking is what a distilled critic inherits.
  Labels carry real headroom, concentrated at 5–8 and the tails.

  (each labeled game ≈ 33 games through a batch-1 server at ~59 rps; the
  run's tail serialized to 2 workers as chunks drained — expected). A 50K
  run as-is ≈ 15 days → **server micro-batching is mandatory before any
  scale-up** (GPU mostly idle between single-request round-trips).
- **DECISION (user-confirmed): D4 CLOSED, rollout-label scale-up DEFERRED**
  behind micro-batching or D6 telemetry demand — machinery certified,
  priced, parked. `d4-critic-fullvis` = critic of record.

  and exactly why the signal comparison scores both BCE and AUC.
