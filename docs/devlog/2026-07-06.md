# Session 5: value-head diagnosis arc — conditioning was the disease, twice; policy was just hungry

## Done

- **Value-head diagnostic built and the hypothesis space walked down to the ground** (`anvil/training/diagnose_value.py`: BCE/AUC binned by turn and turns-from-end, calibration, prediction spread; report JSON beside each checkpoint). run1's "at chance" was NOT an averaging artifact: AUC 0.53 flat to the final turn, pred std 0.015, but the ~0.1% of windows where the head committed were near-perfectly calibrated — signal reached the head only at input extremes. Smoking gun for input conditioning, not labels/plumbing.
- **Transform v2 (state scalars to O(1))**: raw magnitudes (lib ~90, turn ~20) drowned life differences in `state_proj`'s linear mix. run2 A/B (identical arch/steps): val value BCE 0.691 → 0.647, calibrated extremes at 7% of windows, valpair lockstep. Policy unchanged (the 0.739 nonpass I got excited about mid-run was small-slice noise — the new per-metric ns in metrics.jsonl caught my own overread).
- **v3 (entity scalars + card-fusion scaling + ID-emb init)**: audit found two more sites of the same disease — entity dynamic features (~10–20×) and, much worse, the CardEncoder fusion concat: unit-L2 Qwen3 text dims (~0.016) beside raw CMC~15 (**~1,000× per-dim**) and N(0,1) ID-emb init (~60×), i.e. an implicit prior of features ≫ identity ≫ text — memorization channel louder than generalization channel, and a confound that would have silently poisoned the Qwen3-vs-BGE ablation. run3: value BCE 0.647 → **0.578**, commits on 17% of windows, AUC up in every bin. Policy again unchanged.
- **Eval power upgrade** (train.py): per-metric ns in every eval row; final eval sweeps 600 batches (~154K windows, nonpass SE ~0.33%; X n ~180 instead of 25).
- **Matchup-entropy measurement from the pilot results** (games.jsonl, 4,622 pairs with 5 decisive games): matchups are strongly non-coin-flip — **21.4% of pairs went 5–0 (coin-flip: 6.2%)**; Laplace-shrunk matchup-prior BCE floor ~0.60. Length-sliced: shortest-third pairs 30% deterministic (floor ~0.57), longest-third (the grind cluster) 15% (floor ~0.61). The model's early-game BCE 0.55 beats the matchup floor (it also reads hand/draw info); its late-game BCE sits AT the floor — board reading absent beyond the prior.
- **pilot-run4: the full-epoch discriminator (113K steps, ~90 min nice-19).** Fresh data split the heads cleanly: **policy/target/X were data-starved** — honest 0.964 (raw 0.965), **nonpass 0.708 → 0.837**, target-slot 0.965, **X 0.41 → 0.67** (n=173), valpair 0.966 = still zero held-out-matchup gap. **Value was NOT**: BCE 0.575 (vs 0.578), binned shape identical to run3 — final-turn AUC 0.684, late bins at the matchup floor.
- **ADR-0006**: 500K generation is default-on behind fork hardening, with the pre-registered stopping rule (nonpass improvement < 2×SE at doubling AND rare-label heads plateaued → stop). Value BCE excluded from the rule (measured: not data-limited).

## Broke / surprised me

- **The value signal is front-loaded and stayed front-loaded through every fix.** Confidence *decreases* with turn — the model rides an early-visible prior (matchup + own hand) it can't update, rather than reading boards. v3 lifted every bin but kept the shape; the full epoch didn't move it at all.
- **The pre-registered trigger fired but needs an honest refinement.** "Full epoch doesn't fix late-game ⇒ labels are the suspect" was too coarse: at the final turn, truly decided states have nearly noise-free labels, yet AUC is 0.68. Label noise (grind-game entropy — real, measured) can't be the whole residual. The remaining gap decomposes into (1) hidden-information entropy (wins from hand are invisible to the loser's information set — irreducible for ANY value function, Ante's domain eventually), (2) board/combat-math reading the trunk hasn't learned (architecture/inductive-bias question — can attention do lethal arithmetic over 150 entities?), (3) grind label noise (the part rollout-labeled targets would fix). Separating (1) from (2) needs rollout ground truth — the fork API again.
- Mid-run cross-run comparisons under different cosine schedules are near-meaningless; final-vs-final on the big slice only.
