# M3 plan — strength on a community-integrated platform

**Date:** 2026-07-17. **Anchors:** [m3-candidates.md](m3-candidates.md) (the pre-planning menu this plan resolves); [ADR-0020](../decisions/ADR-0020-m2-closeout.md) (M2 closeout); [ADR-0019](../decisions/ADR-0019-d6-donewhen4-resolution.md) (done-when #4, veto-drift lever list); [run3-veto-forensics.md](run3-veto-forensics.md) (commander-tax hypothesis); [ADR-0017](../decisions/ADR-0017-run2-entropy-collapse.md) (guards, hinge floor, lr bracket); [ADR-0018](../decisions/ADR-0018-ruleset-scope-clarification.md) (features-before-breadth); [discord-ai-plotting-survey.md](discord-ai-plotting-survey.md) + the #ai-plotting thread (2026-07-16/17); [upstream-worklist.md](upstream-worklist.md); [ADR-0021](../decisions/ADR-0021-m3-opening-sequence.md) (this plan's seed); [m2-rl-plan.md](m2-rl-plan.md) (the pattern this doc follows).
**Question answered:** what M3 builds, in what order, and what closes it — such that the RL loop's known structural leak is fixed at its cause, the guarded recipe is scaled to its honest ceiling, and the fork returns to upstream's tip carrying the community's determinism work with it.

## The milestone in one paragraph

M2 closed with the loop proven: a guarded V-trace chain whose final checkpoint beats the teacher it imitated (+6.75pp ± 2.31, t=2.92, Ante-corrected at 2,000 games — ADR-0019). It also named the next problem precisely: veto drift (25→41% across run-3) with a forensically-supported cause candidate — **the observation lacks commander tax, so recasts are priced at printed cost** (76% of iter-013 vetoes are `unpayable`; 73% of commander vetoes are post-first-cast). Meanwhile the community moment arrived: our #ai-plotting introduction drew manabrew's determinism-collaboration enthusiasm and maintainer Hanmac filed [forge#11260](https://github.com/Card-Forge/forge/issues/11260) (per-Game ID counters, self-assigned + two maintainers) within a day. M3 is a **split milestone with an RL spine**: fix the veto cause and scale the guarded recipe (the strength program), while the upstream track — #11260 support, the joint determinism PR, the protocol review, the endstep evaluation — runs in parallel, absorbing community latency. The close is the #11161 rebase: one dataset-boundary event that folds our merged #11203 fixes, the determinism hooks, and upstream's drift back into the fork, gated by forkcheck.

## Settled decisions (user 2026-07-17 + carried rules, consequences here)

1. **Identity: split, RL spine.** RL science is the milestone's spine — it's hot, diagnosed, and cheap (~45 min/iteration, guards standing). Upstream work is a parallel track whose pacing is set by community latency (maintainer replies, review cycles), not by us; it never blocks the spine.
2. **The #11161 rebase is the closing event, not the opener.** Let the joint determinism PR and #11260 land upstream first; one rebase then picks up everything (#11203 fixes return, `thisTurnCast` rework, pre-shuffle sort, per-Game counters). Corollary: the fork stays pinned through all M3 RL runs; the pre-shuffle sort does NOT land fork-side early (it's behavior-invariant but trajectory-changing — dataset-boundary only).
3. **Grindstone is the flex slot** (the D5-in-M2 pattern): seeded by the 34 never-cast blind-spot cards, picked up if the spine tracks well, dropped without ceremony if not.
4. **Veto drift is attacked cause-first.** The commander-tax observation gap is the leading candidate and it's a *feature fix*, not reward shaping. The reward veto penalty (needs a §3d pin), per-head entropy floors, and X-inclusion refinement stay in reserve for the residual — and the counterspell-hold cluster gets examined before anything is penalized (holding counterspell mana and passing is often *correct* Magic).
5. **#11260 is maintainer-led; we support, we don't implement.** Contribution = design input (the GameCopier/GameSnapshot counter-copy constraint from #11203, the sibling-statics enumeration) + branch verification via forkcheck. Our authored surface narrows to the remaining hooks: ThreadLocal MyRandom, pre-shuffle sort, tests.
6. **Nothing unverified gets co-signed upstream.** Precedent set 2026-07-17: manabrew's Fireball SVar claim probed clean (`FireballRaiseCostTest`, fork `023e8c5da9` — does not reproduce) and is excluded from the joint PR. Every claim in the joint PR carries a test.
7. **Track C expressiveness stays out** except what RL telemetry indicts (X-inclusion refinement is queued; AR combat decoder and mode heads only if a run demonstrates the need). Pool breadth waits per ADR-0018's features-before-breadth rule.
8. **Endstep concurrency is evaluate-don't-assume.** manabrew reports "very good" improvement (their PR #493); true in-JVM concurrency could reshape worker economics (8×2g JVMs → threads), but our chunk mechanism's crash isolation is load-bearing — adopt only if a measured arm wins under our failure-census requirements.
9. **Standing rules carry unchanged:** ADR-0017 guards (KL/entropy/veto triplines, hinge floor, lr bracket [1e-5, 2e-5] known-survivable at 2e-5-with-guards), arms with `--ante` corrected reads for milestone claims, `forkcheck -grpc` BC-ckpt gate for engine bumps (ADR-0020 amendment), babysit-with-wakeups for overnight runs.

## Deliverables

The spine (D1→D2) is strictly ordered; the upstream track (D3) runs parallel from day one; D4 closes.

**D1 — Commander-tax fix + veto instrumentation.** (a) Derivability check: commander tax is 2×(prior casts from command zone) — establish whether the existing obs stream lets the featurizer derive it reader-side (cast history + zone provenance are logged; expectation: **yes, no corpus regeneration**; if not, it's a fork-side obs field + the fix waits on nothing but a worker rebuild — still no regeneration, the feature only matters at serve/train time going forward). (b) Feature lands in featurizer+loader with a `load_compat` shim (the D5 pattern: new input rows/dims init fresh, old ckpts load unchanged). (c) Checkpoint-boundary choice — inject into the RL chain from iter-012 with fresh feature weights vs a brief BC-side value/policy fine-tune first: decided in-deliverable (user lean 2026-07-17: get the feature in early rather than deferring it); pilot cheaply if the first path wobbles, pick by first-attempt veto response and arms tripwire (a tie is acceptable; the target is the veto trajectory under RL pressure, not instant winrate). (d) **First-attempt veto rate** joins monitor.jsonl and census (chains currently inflate the headline rate; re-ask made the old metric semantics-shifting) — this is the metric done-when #1 reads. Done: post-first-cast commander vetoes collapse in a forensics re-read on fresh RL games.

**D2 — Guarded scaling run(s).** Run-4 from the best D1 checkpoint: lr within the bracket, iteration count no longer capped by fear (run-3 proved the guards work — a bad direction costs ~10 minutes, not ~5 hours), fresh seeds, `-reask`, per-4-iteration arms, 2,000-game Ante-corrected read on the final/best checkpoint. The question this deliverable answers: **where is the current recipe's ceiling?** Cumulative KL from init was ~0.05 in run-1 (lr barely moved the policy) and run-3 gained +4pp in 13 iterations — there is unspent headroom between "barely moved" and "guard halt." Reserve levers if veto drift persists post-tax-fix, in order: examine the counterspell-hold cluster, then the §3d-pinned reward veto penalty, then per-head entropy floors. Watch items carry: x-factor inclusion (ent_x), chain-depth ≥4 census, bf16 pick-match tripwire.

**D3 — Upstream track (parallel, community-paced).** (a) **#11260 support**: the design-input comment (counter-must-copy constraint, sibling statics: SpellAbility, SpellAbilityStackInstance, StaticAbility, ReplacementEffect, Trigger, CombatView), then forkcheck verification of whatever branch appears. (b) **Joint determinism PR** with khaliostr/manabrew: ThreadLocal MyRandom (seedable per worker) + deterministic pre-shuffle ordering + tests; coordinated so it complements #11260 rather than colliding; we author or co-author per the Discord thread. (c) **manabrew protocol #445 review** against the 109-callback decision-surface census — deliverable is a gap list posted to their PR, not interop work. (d) **Endstep concurrency evaluation**: a measured arm (throughput, RAM, failure census under kill-a-worker) on their patch set; adopt/decline with numbers. (e) Small goodwill items as they arise (FireballRaiseCostTest offered upstream; never-cast sweep shared with LordOfThePigs — already offered in-thread).

**D4 — The #11161 rebase (closing dataset-boundary event).** Rebase the fork onto upstream tip: drop the fork-local #11203 copies (merged upstream), reconcile the residual-divergence copier work against SpellAbility-typed `thisTurnCast`, take the pre-shuffle sort + whatever determinism work landed (#11260, joint PR), re-baseline forkcheck (`-grpc` with the BC checkpoint per the amended gate protocol), fresh determinism/throughput baselines, worklist swept. Every RL run after D4 is on the new base; every run before it is on the pinned fork — no mixing.

**Flex — Grindstone seeding (§6).** First drill templates from the 34 never-cast blind spots (cards with millions of candidate windows and zero casts — no imitation signal exists, RL-exploration-only): drill = provenance-traced position where the blind-spot card is castable and plausibly correct, generated from real corpus games. Scope-boxed to template extraction + a first drill batch + a measurement of whether drilled positions move the never-cast rate under RL; the full drill economy is M4+.

**Infra riders** (attach to whichever deliverable touches them first): VRAM elasticity (task #12: per-phase seg autotune + OOM-catch-retry) before the next long run; guard-halt push notification from the driver; single-JVM workers only if D3(d) evaluates well.

## Risks and open questions

- **The tax fix may not be the whole veto story.** The forensics support is strong (73% post-first-cast concentration) but correlational; the counterspell-hold cluster and non-commander unpayables remain. D1's forensics re-read is the honest test; the reserve levers are ordered and none fire blind.
- **New-feature injection into a trained chain.** Fresh feature weights mid-RL could transiently destabilize (the policy suddenly sees a new input); the D1 pilot prices BC-fine-tune-first vs direct injection. Guards catch a bad transient either way.
- **Community latency and scope creep.** The upstream track's pace isn't ours to set; the spine never waits on it. Scope discipline: small PRs, every claim tested, no interop commitments beyond the #445 gap list.
- **#11260 design drift.** Maintainers may choose a design that doesn't serve determinism as well (e.g., counters not copied on snapshot). The comment gets the constraint on the record early; forkcheck verification catches regressions before the rebase imports them.
- **Rebase scale.** 57+ commits of upstream drift and growing; the `thisTurnCast` rework touches our residual-divergence class. Budget real archaeology time; forkcheck is the gate and fresh baselines are part of the deliverable, not optional.
- **Recipe ceiling unknown.** D2 may find the ceiling quickly (KL budget exhausts, arms plateau) — that's a finding, not a failure; it prices the next lever (full-vis critic in the loop, rollout labels under micro-batching) for M4.
- **Endstep adoption tension.** Threads win RAM/startup but lose per-game crash isolation; our failure census depends on process boundaries. The evaluation must test the failure path, not just the throughput path.

## M3 is done when

1. **The veto cause is resolved:** post-first-cast commander vetoes are eliminated in a forensics re-read on fresh RL games, and first-attempt veto rate is at-or-below the D5 baseline and non-increasing across an accepted run.
2. **A guarded run beats the M2 record:** some run-4+ checkpoint beats iter-012 outside noise on the paired 2,000-game Ante-corrected read (the M2-close read: 0.5213 ± 0.0110 corrected, +6.75pp vs the D5 BC arm).
3. **The upstream track has landed its committed surface:** the #11260/joint-PR determinism work is submitted upstream with our tests and forkcheck verification, and the #445 review gap list is delivered.
4. **The rebase is complete:** fork on upstream tip past #11161, fork-local #11203 copies dropped, `forkcheck -grpc` BC-ckpt gate green, fresh baselines recorded, worklist swept.

Clause 2 is the spine's gate; clauses 3–4 are the platform's. The flex slot has no clause — Grindstone reports what it finds or stays silent.
